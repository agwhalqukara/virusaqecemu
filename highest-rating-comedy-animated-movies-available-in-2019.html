Highest Rating Comedy/Animated Movies Available In 2019

The year 2019 is being considered the year of movie enthusiasts with many super cool movies already released or in the pipeline. The year has been amazing for movie fans so far, with releases such as Captain Marvel, Avengers: Endgame and multiple others which have done great on the American box office.

You can watch many of these productions through online movie and social media platforms and/or applications using dependable internet or catch them via cable TV services such as provided by TV packages offered by Spectrum. Today I will share with you a few comedy/animated movie releases that would surely make your day. So without further delay let’s get you acquainted with some truly entertaining films that you can watch with family and friends this year.

How to Train Your Dragon: The Hidden World

Cast: Jay Baruchel, Gerard Butler, Kieron Elliott, America Ferrera, Craig Ferguson, Kit Harington, Justin Rupple Robin Atkin Downes, F. Murray Abraham, Cate Blanchett, Julia Emelin, Jonah Hill, Gideon Emery, Christopher Mintz-Plasse, Kristen Wiig and others

Plot: The movie is set in a time period that is a year ahead of the events which occurred during the previous film. Hiccup, Toothless and other dragon-riders continue to rescue the captured dragons – struggling to bring them to Berk. Their efforts however result in the island becoming overpopulated with dragons, prompting Hiccup to find the Hidden World – a safe territory for dragons spoken of by his late father Stoick. In the meantime, a white female dragon is captured by the warlords and given to the dragon hunter named Grimmel the Grisly – Grimmel uses her as bait to capture Toothless who the warlords intend to employ as an alpha. Watch Hiccup and Toothless bring you some amazing adventures as they battle their way out of the evil clutches of Grimmel the Grisly and strive to survive along with other dragons outside the world of humans.

IMDb Rating: 7.5

The LEGO Movie 2: The Second Part

Cast: Chris Pratt, Stephanie Beatriz, Brooklynn Prince, Alison Brie, Elizabeth Banks, Charlie Day, Nick Offerman, Maya Rudolph, Will Ferrell, Jadon Sand, Channing Tatum, Will Arnett, Tiffany Haddish, Jonah Hill, Richard Ayoade and others

Plot: The simple folks of Bricksburg face a new threat. The LEGO DUPLO invaders show up from outer space unleashing destruction upon everything that lies in their path. A battle rages on – Bricksburg fights to defeat the foreign invaders and restore peace and harmony to the LEGO Universe. Emmet, Lucy, Batman and others embark on a journey to faraway unexplored worlds – a test of their courage, creativity and teamwork – but they must destroy the enemy.

IMDb Rating: 6.7

What Men Want

Cast: Taraji P. Henson, Mathias Alvarez, Kristen Ledlow, Jason Jones, Chris Witaske, Max Greenfield, Paul Brian Johnson, Brian Bosworth, Kellan Lutz, Kausar Mohammed, Josh Brener and others

Plot: Following denial of a well-deserved promotion of sorts, Ali Davis who works as a successful sports agent wonders what else she must do to become successful in a man’s world. The answer to her curiosity comes from a chance encounter with a psychic – the psychic makes her drink a concoction of queer taste that bestows upon Ali the power to hear men’s thoughts. With this newfound ability, she begins to turn tables on her male colleagues as she competes to sign the next basketball superstar named Jamal Barry. She soon realizes though she could hear Jamal’s thoughts she never listened attentively. When she meets Jamal next at her hangout spot she asks him what he really wanted. Jamal opens up to her and tells he wants to be with his own people. Ali advises him to follow his heart which results in Jamal deciding to stay in the US – he moves onto become the number 1 pick for an NBA team while Ali gets promoted and eventually sets up her own agency.

IMDb Rating: 5.1

Isn’t It Romantic

Cast: Rebel Wilson, Alex Kis, Adam Devine, Liam Hemsworth, Betty Gilpin, Priyanka Chopra, Brandon Scott Jones, Jennifer Saunders, and others

Plot: Natalie is one of the architects in New York who works hard to be recognized at her workplace. But her prospects look grim – she may never end up designing a skyscraper rather only “deliver coffee and bagels”. Things get a little too weird for her when she gets knocked out and is left unconscious following a mugging incident in the subway. Natalie wakes up only to find herself in an alternate reality. As cynical as she had always been about love, with a strong hatred for romantic comedies, Natalie’s worst nightmare comes true – she discovers she did not have a choice but to find true love if she wanted to step back into her own reality – in a way she comes to play the leading lady in a romantic comedy before she comes back to the real world – but when she does return she is a new woman who has left behind her cynicism and her low self-esteem.

IMDb Rating: 5.9

Fighting with My Family

Cast: Dwayne Johnson, Lena Headey, Florence Pugh, Tori Ellen Ross, Nick Frost,Jack Lowden, Olivia Bernstone, Leah Harvey, Mohammad Amiri, Vince Vaughn and others

Plot: Paige and Zak are born in a family where everybody is a wrestler. Both of them find themselves gifted with the life-time opportunity to try their luck in World Wrestling Entertainment. Paige finds herself caught up in a dilemma wherein she has to make one of the most difficult choices – she must choose between her career as a wrestler and her family. At the same time, she faces a highly competitive environment in the industry. She pushes herself and proves her mettle in the world of wrestling. The experience transforms her person making her a strong individual not just a wrestling star.

IMDb Rating: 7.1
