Klay Thompson’s Contract

At the beginning of his professional career in 2011 with the Golden State Warriors, Klay signed a standard 4-year / $9,742,480 rookie contract, a $2.32 million contract extension in the 2013-2014 season, and a $2.32 million contract extension in 2014-2015. It was also in the 2014-2015 season that the Golden State Warriors won their first NBA Championship since 1975.

Klay Thompson then signed a 4-year / $68,979,450 contract extension with the Warriors. The Warriors did not win the championship during the 2016 season, however, with the power combo of Klay Thompson and Stephen Curry, they were able to win the 2017 and 2018 titles.

In  2019 Klay signed a 5-year / $189.9 million maximum contract that will see him play for the Golden State Warriors till 2024. Unfortunately, he sustained an anterior cruciate ligament injury in the penultimate game of the 2019 NBA Finals, which kept him off the court throughout the 2019–2020 season. He also missed out on the  2020–2021 season with a torn Achilles tendon he sustained while still recovering from his ACL injury.

He finally returned to court in January 2022 after missing out on play for 31 months. He went on to help the Warriors with their fourth championship in eight seasons by securing the 2022 title. He has so far won four championship rings and made it to the NBA All-Star game five times.

Where did Klay Thompson go to College

Klay Thomson attended Washington State University (WSU), where he played his collegiate career with the Cougars men’s basketball team. As a freshman, he started all 33 games for Tony Bennett, the head coach, and averaged 12.5 points per game. He was named to the Pac-10 All-Freshman Team and Collegehoops.net All-Freshman Honorable Mention Team.

In the seasons that followed, Thompson continued to show his strength on the court. He led the Cougars to the Great Alaska Shootout Championship in his sophomore year as he was named its Most Outstanding Player after he scored a tournament single game record of 43 points in its championship. He was also named to the All-Pac-10 First Team after he became the third fastest Cougar to reach 1,000 points.

Thompson finished his sophomore season with an average of 19.6 points. He also finished his third year by setting Washington State University’s single season scoring record with 733 points. He is also the institution’s 3rd all-time leading scorer.

On the 18th of January 2020, Washington State retired Thompson’s jersey, No. 1, making him the second WSU men’s basketball player to be honored this way and the seventh WSU athlete whose number has been retired.
