Know About Suzanne Malveaux; Partner, CNN, Parents, Father, Child

While the rumors about Suzanne Malveaux’s sexual orientation have been hitting the search engines for quite some time now, the news sources have claimed of she having a same-sex partner. Besides, she is believed to co-parent a child with her companion as well.

Suzanne Malveaux is an American television news journalist who formerly served as a CNN White House correspondent. She began her media career in the early 1990s.

Insights on Suzanne Malveaux’s Bio & Family: Age, Father, Mother

The 53-years-of-age media personality was born as Suzanne Maria Malveaux on 4 December 1966, to her parents, Floyd Joseph Malveaux and Myrna Maria Ruiz.

Suzanne’s late father, Floyd, was a doctor who became the dean of the College of Medicine at Howard University. He then served as the executive director of the Merck Childhood Asthma Network and was a founder of Howard University’s National Human Genome Center. Floyd waved goodbye to the world on 1 January 2020.

Suzanne Malveaux remembers her late father on 12 January 2020 (Picture: Suzanne’s Twitter)

Meantime, her late mother, Myrna, formerly served as a schoolteacher. She passed away on 13 April 2018, after fighting with ALS (Amyotrophic lateral sclerosis) for a long time.

Her Siblings, Education

Suzanne’s family includes her three siblings, including an identical twin. Her twin sister, Suzette M. Malveaux, is a professor at the Columbus School of Law, Catholic University of America. Likewise, her brother, Courtney M. Malveaux is a commissioner of the Virginia Department of Labor and Industry, and her another brother, Gregory F. Malveaux, is an associate professor of English at Montgomery College.

Speaking about her education, Suzanne graduated from Centennial High School in Ellicott City, Maryland in 1984. Later, she graduated with an A.B. cum laude in Sociology from Harvard College. Eventually, Suzanne received a master’s degree in broadcasting from the Columbia University Graduate School of Journalism in 1991.

Her Career, Net Worth

From her long-haul media career, Suzanne Malveaux earns quite a compelling sum. Her net worth is estimated to swirl between $2 million to $5 million.

Suzanne initiated her media career as a general assignment reporter for the New England Cable News from 1992 to 1996. After that, she moved to Washington, D.C. and served for NBC affiliate WRC-TV as a self-claimed ‘rock and roll’ reporter reporting local and crime news from 1996 to 1999.

Suzanne Malveaux reporting on CNN (Picture: CNN)

She joined CNN in 2002 after reporting at the NBC News for three years from 1999. Suzanne served as a CNN White House correspondent as a primary substitute to Wolf Blitzer in The Situation Room. She also co-anchored the CNN international news program Around The World and editions of CNN Newsroom.

Some Interesting Facts About Suzanne Malveaux

  • Suzanne grew up in a New Orleans-based family of Louisiana Creoles, whose roots are of French, Spanish, and African descent.
  • As a White House correspondent, her coverage of presidential trips overseas has taken her to Europe, the Balkans, Southeast Asia, Africa, Australia, Latin America, and the Middle East.
